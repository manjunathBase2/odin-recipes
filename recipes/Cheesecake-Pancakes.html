Cheesecake Pancakes

Description

These cheesecake pancakes are a delicious and indulgent breakfast treat. They’re made with cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla extract, and they’re topped with a sweet and tangy raspberry sauce. They’re perfect for a special occasion or when you’re craving something sweet for breakfast.

Cheesecake pancakes are a great way to start your day on a sweet note. They’re easy to make and can be customized with your favorite toppings. Try them with fresh berries, whipped cream, or a drizzle of maple syrup for a delicious breakfast treat.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup all-purpose flour
  2. 2 tablespoons sugar
  3. 1 teaspoon baking powder
  4. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  5. 1/4 teaspoon salt
  6. 1 cup buttermilk
  7. 1/4 cup cream cheese, softened
  8. 1 egg
  9. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  10. 1/4 cup butter, melted
  11. 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  12. 1/4 cup sugar
  13. 1/4 cup water

Steps

    Step 1

  • In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

    • Step 2

  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, cream cheese, egg, and vanilla extract until smooth.

    • Step 3

  • Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.

    • Step 4

  • Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and brush with melted butter.

    • Step 5

  • Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet and cook until bubbles form on the surface, about 2-3 minutes.

    • Step 6

  • Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until golden brown.

    • Step 7

  • Repeat with the remaining batter, brushing the skillet with more butter as needed.

    • Step 8

  • In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, and water.

    • Step 9

  • Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5-7 minutes, until the raspberries have broken down and the sauce has thickened.

    • Step 10

  • Remove from heat and strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds.

    • Step 11

  • Serve the pancakes warm with the raspberry sauce drizzled on top.
